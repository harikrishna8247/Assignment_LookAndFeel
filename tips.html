Common Houseplants Issues and Tips

Plant Name Issue Troubleshooting Tip
Fern Over-watering Allow soil to dry out between waterings
Spider Plant Root rot Ensure proper drainage and avoid over-watering
Rubber Tree Leaf loss Check for drafts and maintain consistent watering
Aloe Vera Leaf wilting Adjust watering frequency and provide adequate sunlight
Peace Lily Brown leaf tips Use distilled water and increase humidity
Pothos Yellowing leaves, over-watering Allow soil to dry out between waterings and improve drainage
Devil's Ivy Browning leaves, over-watering Reduce watering frequency and ensure proper drainage
ZZ Plant Yellowing leaves, over-watering Allow soil to dry out completely between waterings
Philodendron Brown leaf tips, root rot Trim affected leaves and adjust watering frequency
Chinese Money Plant Brown spots on leaves If brown spots appear, it could indicate that the plant is receiving too much direct sunlight. Move the plant to a location with more indirect light.
Calathea Brown leaf edges or yellowing leaves To prevent brown edges, maintain high humidity by misting the leaves or placing a humidifier nearby. Ensure the plant is not exposed to direct sunlight, as this can cause leaf damage. Monitor watering to avoid overwatering, which can lead to yellowing leaves.
Monstera Brown leaf edges or yellowing leaves Similar to Calathea, maintain high humidity and avoid direct sunlight. Ensure proper drainage to prevent overwatering, which can cause yellowing leaves. Trim brown edges with clean scissors to improve aesthetics.
Parlor Palm Browning leaves or yellowing leaves Check soil moisture regularly and adjust watering frequency accordingly. Ensure the plant is not placed in direct sunlight, which can cause leaf burn. Trim any brown or yellow leaves to promote healthy growth.
